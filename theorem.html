Theorem-1[Linear Property]

Suppose f1(s) and f2(s) are Laplace forms of F1(t) and F2(t) respectively. Then L{c1F1(t)+c2F2(t)}=c1L[F1(t)]+c2L[F2(t)], where c1 and c2 are any constants.

Theorem-2 [First Shifting Theorem]

If L[f(t)]=f(s), then

L{eatF(t)}=f(s-a)

This theorem can also be restated as: If f(s) is the Laplace transform of eatF(t) and a is any real or complex number, then f(s+a) is the Laplace transform of e-atF(t). This is to say,

f(s)=L[F(t)] ⇒ f(s+a) = LeatF(t)

Theorem-4 [Existence of Laplace transform]

If F(t) is a function of class A, then Laplace transform of F(t) exists.

Suppose F(t) is a piece-wise continuous in every finite interval and is of exponential order a as t →∞. Then f(s) exists ∀ s >A,i.e.Laplace transform exists.
